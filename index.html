01

Origins and the Mark of Fate

Kratos

Kratos was forged in the brutal crucible of Sparta, born as a demigod son to Zeus, the King of Olympus, and a mortal woman named Callisto. Fearing the wrath of his jealous queen, Hera, Zeus hid Kratos and his younger brother, Deimos, in plain sight, leaving them to be raised under the unforgiving Spartan regime.

Destiny, however, would not allow them a quiet life. An ancient prophecy foretold the doom of Mount Olympus at the hands of a "marked warrior." When Ares, the God of War, and Athena, the Goddess of Wisdom, descended upon Sparta to preempt this threat, they targeted Deimos due to his unusual, fiery birthmark. Kratos fought fiercely to save his brother, but he was easily brushed aside, leaving him with a scar over his right eye and a lifetime of burning guilt.

Deimos was dragged into the underworld, imprisoned and tortured by Thanatos, the God of Death. Believing his brother was dead, Kratos tattooed a replicate of Deimos's birthmark across his own body—a silent oath to never show weakness again.

02

The Covenant of Blood and the Ghost of Sparta

Ares

As an adult, Kratos rose to become a legendary Spartan general, leading his armies with ruthless efficiency. His hubris met its match on a blood-soaked battlefield against a horde of invading barbarians. Facing certain death under the hammer of the Barbarian King, Kratos screamed a desperate plea to the heavens:

"Ares! Destroy my enemies, and my life is yours!"

— Kratos, on the battlefield
The Blood Oath

The God of War accepted the bargain. Descending in a storm of fire, Ares decimated the barbarian horde and bestowed upon Kratos the Blades of Chaos—twin curved daggers forged in the depths of Hades, bound to Kratos' forearms by searing chains fused directly to his flesh. Kratos became Ares' personal executioner, leading brutal campaigns across Greece in a blind frenzy of slaughter.

This unchecked violence reached a horrific climax during a raid on a village temple dedicated to Athena. Though a village oracle warned him not to enter, Kratos burst through the doors, his vision clouding with an unnatural, blood-red battle lust conjured by Ares. He slaughtered everyone inside. Only when the frenzy subsided did the smoke clear to reveal his victims: his wife, Lysandra, and his young daughter, Calliope.

Ares had orchestrated the horror, intending to sever Kratos' last ties to humanity to mold him into the ultimate, unfeeling killing machine. Crushed by unbearable grief and rage, Kratos rejected his master. As he fled the burning temple, the village oracle cursed him: the ashes of his butchered family were permanently bound to his skin, staining him a ghostly, ash-white. From that night on, he was condemned to walk the earth as the Ghost of Sparta, carrying the physical remains of his sins for all to see.

03

Service to the Pantheon and the Hunt for Ares

Athena

For ten agonizing years, Kratos served the other gods of Olympus as a mercenary, desperately hoping they would rid him of the agonizing nightmares that plagued his mind. He undertook impossible tasks, from slaying the multi-headed Hydra of the Aegean Sea to battling the dark forces threatening the sun god Helios. During these years, Kratos even found his brother Deimos in the Domain of Death. Though they briefly reunited, Deimos perished in battle against Thanatos, fueling Kratos' burning resentment toward the scheming Olympian gods who had stolen his family twice over.

Eventually, Athena offered him a final path to redemption. Ares had gone rogue, waging a siege of terror against Athena's namesake city, Athens. Because Zeus had forbidden the gods from warring directly against one another, Athena tasked Kratos with slaying the God of War.

To kill a god, Kratos required the ultimate weapon: Pandora's Box, hidden within a massive temple strapped to the back of the wandering Titan, Cronos, in the Desert of Lost Souls. Kratos braved lethal traps, solved ancient puzzles, and fought through legions of the undead to claim the box. Ares, sensing Kratos' success, hurled a wooden pillar across Greece, impaling Kratos and sending him screaming into the depths of Hades.

Refusing to let death stop his vengeance, Kratos clawed his way back to the mortal realm. He opened Pandora's Box, growing to titanic size and absorbing its divine energies. After an epic clash that shattered the psychic and physical plains, Kratos drove the Blade of the Gods through Ares' chest, ending the reign of the first God of War.

Yet, the gods' promises proved hollow. Athena revealed that while they could forgive his sins, no power—divine or mortal—could ever erase the memories of his family's slaughter. Devastated and seeking peace in the cold embrace of death, Kratos threw himself from the highest cliff in Greece. But the gods would not let their favorite weapon die. Athena pulled him from the sea and elevated him to the vacant throne, crowning Kratos as the new God of War.

01

The Betrayal of Zeus

Zeus

As a god, Kratos' anger only intensified. He turned his back on Olympus, finding solace only in the company of his Spartan soldiers, whom he guided to bloody conquests across the Aegean. Fearing that Kratos would destroy the mortal world—and terrified of the ancient prophecy that a son of Zeus would overthrow his father—Zeus decided to end Kratos' reign.

During a siege of Rhodes, Zeus tricked Kratos into channeling his divine essence into the Blade of Olympus, supposedly to defeat a giant colossus. Stripped of his godhood, a weakened Kratos was easily struck down by Zeus, who ran him through with the very blade he had empowered.

Zeus's Betrayal
Kratos (God) ──► Pours power into Blade of Olympus ──► Weakened/Mortal │ Zeus (King) ◄── Stabs and kills Kratos ◄───────────────────────────┘

As Kratos' soul dragged toward the underworld, he was rescued by Gaia, the mother of the Titans. Seeking revenge on the Olympians for their defeat in the First Great War, Gaia healed Kratos and set him on a new path: he must find the Sisters of Fate, who controlled the threads of destiny, and use their looms to travel back in time to the moment of his betrayal.

Kratos traveled to the Island of Creation, brutally slaying mythological heroes like Theseus and Perseus who stood in his path. He conquered the Sisters of Fate, seized control of the Loom of Destiny, and rewrote history, returning to Rhodes to fight Zeus. Kratos gained the upper hand and was poised to execute his father, but Athena threw herself in front of the blade, sacrificing her life to save Zeus. With her dying breath, she revealed the ultimate truth: Zeus was indeed Kratos' father, and the cycle of patricide that ruled Greek mythology was doomed to repeat itself.

Unmoved by her sacrifice, Kratos returned to the Loom of Fate once more. He pulled the Titans out of the past and brought them into the present, launching a full-scale invasion of Mount Olympus.

Along his path of destruction, Kratos encountered Pandora, a soul created to act as the key to unlocking the Flame of Olympus that guarded Pandora's Box. Kratos saw a reflection of his lost daughter Calliope in her, and desperately tried to find another way to open the box without sacrificing her. However, driven by her own destiny, Pandora threw herself into the flame.

When Kratos opened the box once more, he found it completely empty. Undeterred, he engaged Zeus in a final, brutal duel. The physical battle soon turned inward, forcing Kratos to confront the dark chambers of his own mind, his guilt, and the spectral memories of his family. Deep within his psyche, Kratos discovered the power of Hope—the very force he had unwittingly absorbed when he first opened Pandora's Box years ago, which had been buried under his lifetime of hate.

Awakening with this power, Kratos beat Zeus to death with his bare hands, ending the age of Olympus.

Athena's astral form appeared, demanding that Kratos return the power of Hope so she could use it to rebuild the world in her own image. Realizing that the gods would only continue their cycle of manipulation, Kratos refused. Instead, he drove the Blade of Olympus through his own chest, releasing Hope into what remained of humanity and denying Athena her prize. Bleeding out onto the ruined, flooded peak of Olympus, Kratos collapsed. Yet, when the screen faded to black, only a trail of blood remained leading over the cliffside, suggesting the Ghost of Sparta had somehow survived his final act of defiance.

01

Midgard and the Quiet Life

Atreus

In the centuries that followed the ruin of Greece, Kratos wandered the earth, eventually settling in the cold, harsh wilderness of Midgard—the realm of the Norse gods. Seeking to bury his violent past, he hid his blades beneath his floorboards and lived as a quiet, stoic mortal. He fell in love with a fierce warrior named Faye (Laufey), and together they had a son, Atreus.

Kratos struggled with fatherhood. Terrified that Atreus would inherit his divine curse of violence, Kratos remained cold, distant, and demanding, hiding both his past and Atreus' own godhood from the boy.

02

The Journey to the Peak

Baldur

God of War (2018) begins with the death of Faye. Her final request was for Kratos and Atreus to scatter her ashes from the highest peak in all the nine realms. Before they could begin their journey, a heavily tattooed stranger named Baldur arrived at their cabin, possessing immense strength and an absolute immunity to pain. Seeking answers and protecting his son, Kratos fought Baldur off in a brutal duel, forcing the father and son to flee into the wild.

Guided by the severed, reanimated head of Mimir, the smartest man alive, Kratos and Atreus navigated the realms. They befriended two bickering Dwarven blacksmiths, Brok and Sindri, who recognized Kratos' weapon—the Leviathan Axe—as a masterpiece they had forged for Faye.

As their journey progressed, the strain of hiding Atreus' divinity took a physical toll on the boy. Suffering from a mysterious illness born from his mind rejecting his own godhood, Atreus collapsed. To save him, Kratos was forced to travel to the frozen depths of Helheim, where his ice-based Leviathan Axe was useless. Retreating home, Kratos dug up his past, retrieving the Blades of Chaos and wrapping the chains back around his arms—fully embracing the monster he once was to save his child.

After Kratos revealed the truth of their godhood, Atreus recovered but grew arrogant, believing himself superior to mortals. Kratos fought to teach him humility, reminding him that being a god is a responsibility, not a license to kill.

Eventually, they confronted Baldur one final time. In the climax of their battle, Atreus used a quiver strap crafted from mistletoe—the only substance capable of breaking the spell of invulnerability cast on Baldur by his mother, the Vanir goddess Freya. Now vulnerable, Baldur was beaten by Kratos. When Baldur attempted to strangle Freya out of pure hatred for the curse of numbness she had placed upon him, Kratos intervened, snapping Baldur's neck to break the cycle of children killing their parents. Freya, devastated by the loss of her son, cursed Kratos and vowed bloody vengeance.

Arriving at the towering peaks of Jotunheim, Kratos and Atreus discovered that the Giants were long dead. They also uncovered an ancient mural detailing their entire journey, proving that Faye had been a Giant herself, and had prophesied their adventure. Atreus was revealed to have another name given to him by the Giants: Loki.

As they scattered Faye's ashes, Kratos noticed a hidden section of the mural depicting a terrifying future: a prophecy showing Kratos dying in the arms of Atreus.

03

Ragnarök and Redemption

Odin

Three years of Fimbulwinter froze Midgard, heralding the arrival of Ragnarök—the prophesied end of the Norse world. Now a teenager, Atreus sought to understand his identity as Loki and his connection to the Giants' souls, while Kratos desperately tried to keep his son safe from the machinations of Odin, the Allfather, and his son Thor.

During their quest to prevent war, Kratos and Atreus rescued a man they believed to be Tyr, the Norse God of War. Tyr became a quiet, pacifist guest in their home. However, this was later revealed to be a devastating deception: the man they rescued was actually Odin in disguise.

The Grand Deception
Odin (Allfather) ──► Disguised as Tyr │ Infiltrates Kratos' camp ◄──────┘ │ Slashes and kills Brok ◄────────┘ ──► Triggers Ragnarök

When the ruse was uncovered, Odin stabbed and killed the beloved dwarf Brok, shattering the alliance and driving his brother, Sindri, into a dark, grieving rage.

Realizing that peace was no longer an option, Kratos stood as a true leader, uniting the armies of the nine realms to march on Asgard. During the siege, Kratos faced Thor in a brutal, earth-shattering rematch. Rather than killing him, Kratos chose to lower his weapon, appealing to Thor's shared experience as a father who wanted better for his children. Inspired, Thor refused Odin's order to kill Kratos. In response, Odin murdered Thor in cold blood.

Kratos, Atreus, and Freya fought and defeated Odin. Refusing to succumb to vengeance, Atreus used his magic to trap Odin's soul in a marble, which Sindri immediately shattered to avenge his brother. Asgard was destroyed, but Kratos ensured the survival of its innocent citizens, choosing protection over mindless slaughter.

In the emotional aftermath, Atreus made the difficult decision to leave Midgard, embarking on a solo journey to find any remaining Giants across the realms. Kratos, now viewing his son as a mature, wise young man, gave him his blessing and said a tearful goodbye.

The Final Shrine

A New Destiny

Left behind, Kratos discovered a final, hidden shrine left by Faye. It depicted a new future: one where Kratos was no longer remembered as the bloodthirsty Ghost of Sparta, but as a beloved, revered god of peace and hope, worshipped by the peoples of the realms he had helped rebuild.

For the first time in his long, painful life, the God of War wept with hope, ready to build a new destiny.
